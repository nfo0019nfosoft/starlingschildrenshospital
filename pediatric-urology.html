Dedicated care for your child’s urinary, kidney, and genital health.

Advanced pediatric technology with child-friendly infrastructure

24/7 pediatric emergency and critical care support

Complete care journey from diagnosis to recovery

Specialised urology care for infants, children & adolescents

Children commonly experience urinary infections, kidney problems, genital abnormalities, and bladder issues. These conditions require specialised pediatric urologists who understand the unique anatomy and needs of growing children.

At Starlings, ourPediatric Allergy Clinic provides accurate diagnosis, child-friendly evaluation, and advanced treatment—ranging from simple medical therapy to minimally invasive procedures.

Our goal is to ensure your child’s urinary system develops normally and stays healthy.

Conditions We Treat

Starlings provides early diagnosis and long-term follow-up for children with neurological needs.

✓ Urinary Tract and Bladder Issues

  • Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs)
  • Bedwetting (Nocturnal Enuresis)
  • Frequent urination
  • Painful urination
  • Bladder dysfunction
  • Vesicoureteral reflux (VUR)

✓ Kidney-Related Concerns

  • Kidney stones
  • Hydronephrosis
  • Congenital kidney abnormalities
  • Recurrent urinary infections
  • Kidney swelling detected during pregnancy (antenatal hydronephrosis)

✓ Genital and Structural Problems

  • Undescended testis
  • Inguinal hernia
  • Hydrocele
  • Phimosis
  • Hypospadias
  • Swelling or pain in private parts
  • Labial adhesions in girls

These conditions are common and often treatable with early evaluation.

✓ Complex Pediatric Urological Conditions

  • Posterior urethral valves (PUV)
  • Obstruction in urine flow
  • Neurogenic bladder
  • Congenital abnormalities requiring surgery

When Should You See a Paediatric Urologist?

Visit Starlings if your child has:

  • Pain while passing urine
  • Repeated urinary infections
  • Wetting the bed after age 5
  • Poor urine flow or dribbling
  • Swelling in scrotum or groin
  • Non-descending testis after 6 months
  • Blood in urine
  • Abdominal or side pain
  • Enlarged kidneys seen on scan

Early evaluation prevents long-term kidney or bladder complications.
